By Salman iStockAnalyst Writer US stocks gained on Monday as upbeat economic data reinforced optimism about the global economic recovery.Below we highlight few companies whose stocks witnessed active trading on Monday.Keryx Biopharmaceuticals NASDAQ: KERX : Shares of the firm soared over 23% on Monday after it announced that the U.S. Food and Drug Administration has given  "fast track" status to perifosine, colorectal cancer therapy.  Keryx is jointly developing perifosine with Candian drugmaker of Aeterna Zentaris and holds the license for perifosine in US, Canada and Mexico.The Fast Track program of the FDA is designed to facilitate the development and expedite the review of new drugs that are intended to treat serious or life threatening conditions and that demonstrate the potential to address unmet medical needs.Fast Track designated drugs ordinarily qualify for priority review, thereby expediting the FDA review process.The companies said they would begin a key phase 3 trial for the in second quarter quarter.The trial is expected to end in second half of 2011, followed by a U.S. launch for perifosine in 2012.Harley Davidson, Inc. NYSE: HOG : Shares of the motrcycle maker jumped 9% after RBC analyst Edward Aaron upped his share price prediction to $36 from $32.In a client note, Aaron wrote that Harley "stands a good chance" of putting an end to its streak of five consecutive earnings dissapointment."Harley is on the cusp of a multi quarter period of progressive improvement in" visibility, demand and the performance of its financial division, he added.Harley Davidson is scheduled to reports first quarter results on April 20."While it is tough to get excited about this number in absolute terms, investors should be encouraged by the read through to March and what it implies for the out quarters, which benefit from much easier comparisons."  
Las Vegas Sands NYSE: LVS : Shares of the casino operator rallied nearly 10% on Monday as several analysts made bullish comment on casino and gaming sector.JPMorgan analyst Carlo Santarelli boosted Melco Crown Entertainment Nasdaq: MPEL from Neutral to Overweight and also lifted its price target on the stock from $5 to $7.Meanwhile, analysts at Goldman Sachs reiterated their Buy rating and $14.50 price target on shares of MGM Mirage NYSE: MGM saying that it "allows portfolio managers to participate in the consumer and business travel recovery along with the company’s own improving corporate finance story."
Zion Bancorp.NYSE: ZION : Collins Stewart on Monday upped Zions Bancorporation to “Hold” from “Sell,” saying that the regional banks like Zion Bancorp.are poised to beat estimates."While investor expectations are undoubtedly high going into the quarter, our sense is that there is still room for the sector to move appreciably higher, at least in the near term," Collins Stewart stated."We believe operating results will be underscored by more pronounced credit stabilization and margin expansion, providing greater visibility on the sector's earnings recovery critical elements to further upside in the group," the analysts added.Shares surged over 5% in afternoon trade.SandRidge Energy Inc. NYSE: SD : Shares of the company lost over 4% in midday trade on Monday.The company announced late Sunday that it has agreed to acquire Arena Resources NYSE: ARD Inc. for about $1.6 billion in cash and stock.The deal values Arena Resources at $40 per share, a 17% premium over Arenas April 1 closing price.SandRidge said that following acquisition, it will become one of the largest producers of West Texas conventional oil and gas.  The transaction is expected to close in June or July, the company said.Tesoro Corp. NYSE: TSO : Shares of the biggest independent refiner on the U.S. West Coast plunged nearly 6%.On Friday, a chemical explosion  at Tesoro’s Anacortes, Washington, plant on April 2, killed five and injured many heightening safety concerns among federal investigators.The explosion prompted the the regulators to start second investigation into the company in just six months.Last year, Washington state regulators fined Tesoro $85,700 for 17 serious safety and health violations, defined as those with potential to cause death or serious physical injury.The settlement reduced the penalty to $12,250 and lowered the number of violations to three.The company reached a settlement with the authorities which reduced the penalty to $12,250 and lowered the number of violations to three.Meanwhile, Caris & Co. analyst Ann Kohler downgraded the stock on Monday, saying that the company's stock likely will underperform its peers amid concerns over the  potential financial impact of the explosion.Disclosure: Author doesn’t own any of the stocks discussed here.Research Related Stocks: Rate this Commentary
