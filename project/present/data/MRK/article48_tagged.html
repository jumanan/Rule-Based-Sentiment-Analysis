By Salman iStockAnalyst Writer US stocks finished lower on Friday as renewed worries over Greece's debt problems spooked investors.The Dow Jones Industrial Average lost 37.19 points or 0.35% to finish at 10,741.98.The S&P 500 dropped 5.92 points or 0.51% to close at 1,159.90.The Nasdaq Composite slipped 16.87 points or 0.71% to 2,374.41.Shares of 3M Co. NYSE: lost $1.71 or 2.04% to close at $81.96.American Express Co. NYSE:
fell 67 cents or 1.63% to $40.33.Bank of America Corp. NYSE: dropped 26 cents or 1.52% to $16.82.Shares of drugmaker finished with significant losses.Merck & Co Inc. NYSE: slumped 39 cents or 1.01% to $38.06.Pfizer Inc. NYSE: declined 32 cents or $1.86% to 16.91.Shares of Wal Mart Stores Inc. NYSE: slipped 60 cents or 1.07% to $55.34.   
Among gainers, shares of Coca Cola Co. NYSE: rallied 80 cents or 1.48% to $54.75.Shares of United Technologies Corp. NYSE: jumped 74 cents or 1.02% to $73.24.Late on Thursday, Palm Inc. NASDAQ: said that its fiscal third quarter net loss narrowed to $18.5 million, or 13 cents a share, compared with a loss of $95 million, or 89 cents a share, in the year ago period.On an adjusted basis, the company lost $102.8 million, or 61 cents a share.Revenue more than tripled to $350 million compared with $90.6 million in the same period last year.Looking ahead, the company said it expects revenue of $150 million for the May quarter.Shares of Palm Inc. sank $1.65 or 29.16% to $4.0025.Shares of SunPower Corp. NASDAQ: plummeted $3.08 or 13.97% to $18.96 after it said Thursday that it expects adjusted 2010 earnings in the range of $1.25 a share to $1.65 a share.For the first quarter, SunPower expects adjusted earnings of 5 cents a share.European stocks settled mixed.The UK FTSE gained 7.50 points or 0.13% to 5,650.12.The German DAX and French CAC decreased 0.50% and 0.32% respectively.Asian stocks finished down.The Nikkei 225 climbed 80.69 points or 0.75% to close at 10,824.72.The Hang Seng index of Hong Kong rose 53.82 points or 0.25% to 21,330.67.NYMEX crude oil for April delivery declined $1.52 or 1.9% to settle at $80.68 a barrel.Disclosure: Author doesn't own any of the stocks discussed here.Research Related Stocks: Rate this Commentary
