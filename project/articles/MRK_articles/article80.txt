Wall Street Climbs As Citigroup Boosts Financials, IBM (NYSE: IBM) Gains
 Thursday, March 11, 2010 5:55 PM
Vote for next session
The next market session will close:
(By Salman - iStockAnalyst Writer)US stocks finished higher on Thursday after Citigroup CEO Vikram Pandit said Thursday that the bank is well positioned to return to sustained profitability.
 The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 44.51 points or 0.42% to finish at 10,611.84. The S&P 500 climbed 4.63 points or 0.40% to close at 1,150.24. The Nasdaq Composite gained 9.51 points or 0.40% to 2,368.46.
 A release by Department of Labor on Thursday showed number of Americans filing first- time claims for unemployment benefits fell 6,000 to 462,00 in the week ending March 6. Economists expected jobless claims to drop to 460,000. The four-week average of seasonally adjusted initial jobless claims, a less volatile gauge, increased 5,000 to 475,500. Continuing claims for the week ending February 27 climbed 37,000 to 4.56 million.
 The Commerce Department reported Thursday that the U.S. trade deficit narrowed by 6.6% in January to $37.29 billion. Economists were looking for a deficit for $41 billion.

 Shares of Walt Disney Co. (NYSE:

 The largest US home improvement retailer Home Depot Inc. (NYSE:

 Shares of Travelers Companies Inc. (NYSE:

 Exxon Mobil Corp. (NYSE: 
) said Thursday that it plans to spend about $27 billion on capital projects in 2010. Exxon spent about $26 billion in 2009. The oil giant said that it planned to spend $25 billion to $30 billion a year through 2014 on capital projects. Shares of the company finished unchanged at $67.22.
 Shares of Citigroup (NYSE: 
) soared 22 cents or 5.56% to $4.18 after chief executive Vikram Pandit said that the firmis on a path toward "sustained profitability" as it sheds risky assets and focuses on emerging markets.
 Shares of Smithfield Foods Inc. (NYSE: 
) rose 24 cents or 1.26% to $19.23 after it reported Thursday that it swung to a third-quarter net income of $37.3 million, or 22 cents a share, compared to a loss of $105.7 million, or 74 cents a share, in the prior-year period. On an adjusted basis the company earned 22 cents a share in the latest period. Revenue dropped to $2.88 billion from $3.35 billion.
 Shares of the company GameStop Corp. (NYSE: 
),  the world's largest video game retailer, surged $1.07 or 5.85% to $19.35 on speculation that the company is a takeover target.
 The National Bureau of Statistics in China reported that the consumer price index rose 2.7% in February from a year earlier, raising concerns about more monetary tightening.
 European stocks settled down. The UK FTSE lost 23.31 points or 0.41% to 5,617.26. The German DAX and French CAC decreased 0.14% and 0.37% respectively.
 Asian stocks finished higher. The Nikkei 225 rose 101.03 points or 0.96% to close at 10,664.95. The Hang Seng index of Hong Kong rose 19.91 points or 0.09% to 21,228.20.
 NYMEX crude oil for April delivery climbed 2 cents to settle at $82.11 a barrel.
