Wall Street Declines On Weak Economic Data, Verizon (NYSE: VZ) Slides
 Wednesday, April 07, 2010 5:34 PM
Vote for next session
The next market session will close:
(By Salman - iStockAnalyst Writer)US stocks finished down on Wednesday as weaker-than-expected consumer credit data prompted many investors to lock in profits.
 The Dow Jones Industrial Average declined 72.47 points or 0.66% to finish at 10,897.52. The S&P 500 slipped 6.99 points or 0.59% to close at 1,182.44. The Nasdaq Composite lost 5.65 points or 0.23% to 2,431.16.
 The Federal Reserve reported Wednesday that total consumer credit fell a seasonally adjusted $11.5 billion, at an annual rate of 5.6%, to $2.448 trillion in February. Economists expected a drop of $0.7 billion.
 Federal Reserve Bank of Kansas City President Thomas Hoenig suggested Wednesday that the central bank should consider starting to raise its key interest rate from record low "sometime soon" to about 1 percent to curb inflation.
 However, Federal Reserve Chairman Ben Bernanke said in a speech today that the U.S. economy still faces significant challenges, indicating that the bank is in no hurry to lift the rates.

 Meanwhile, Greece on Tuesday denied that it is seeking to amend a standby credit plan agreed to during a European Union summit last month to avoid the imposition of tough fiscal conditions by the International Monetary Fund. 
 European stocks settled down. The UK FTSE lost 18.29 points or 0.32% to 5,762.065. The German DAX and French CAC decreased 0.48% and 0.67% respectively.
 Asian stocks finished up. The Nikkei 225 rose 10.51 points or 0.09% to 11,292.83. The Hang Seng index of Hong Kong jumped 391.77 points or 1.82% to $21,928.77.
