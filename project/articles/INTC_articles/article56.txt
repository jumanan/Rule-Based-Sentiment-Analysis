Wall Street Gains As Fed Vows To Keep Rates Low, General Electric (NYSE: GE) Rallies
 Tuesday, March 16, 2010 5:46 PM
Vote for next session
The next market session will close:
(By Salman - iStockAnalyst Writer)US stocks finished higher on Tuesday after the Federal Reserve held benchmark rates at a record low level and reiterated its intent to keep borrowing costs low for an "extended period."
 The Dow Jones Industrial Average climbed 43.83 points or 0.41% to finish at 10,685.98. The S&P 500 gained 8.95 points or 0.78% to close at 1,159.46. The Nasdaq Composite gained 15.80 points or 0.67% to 2,378.01.
 The Federal Open Market Committee left its benchmark interest rate unchanged near zero. The central bank said that the economic conditions are "likely to warrant exceptionally low levels of the federal funds rate for an extended period."
 A release by Department of Commerce on Wednesday showed that housing starts fell 5.9% in February to a seasonally adjusted annual rate of 575,000. Building Permits slipped 1.6% to annual rate of 612,000. Economists expected housing starts to decrease to an annual rate of 601,000 and building permits to decline to 570,000 in February.

 Shares of Limited Brands (NYSE:

 FactSet Research Systems Inc. (NYSE:

 European stocks settled up. The UK FTSE gained 26.58 points or 0.48% to 5,620.43. The German DAX and French CAC increased 1.14% and 1.23% respectively.
 Asian stocks finished lower. The Nikkei 225 fell 30.27 points or 0.28% to close at 10,721.71. The Hang Seng index of Hong Kong slumped 56.17 points or 0.27% to 21,022.93.
