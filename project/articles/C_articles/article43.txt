 SAN FRANCISCO (MarketWatch) -- U.S. financial stocks rose Friday for a fifth consecutive day of gains as financial bellwethers Citigroup Inc. and Bank of America Corp. joined with bond and mortgage insurers to advance the sector by 2% on the week. 
 The Financial Select Sector SPDR Fund 
/quotes/comstock/13*!xlf
, which tracks financial stocks on the S&P 500, closed up 0.1% Friday and up 2% on the week. The sector rose 0.4% on the day and 1.4% on the week. 
/quotes/comstock/13*!c
 shares closed up 1% Friday for a weekly gain of 10.5%, and Bank of America 
/quotes/comstock/13*!bac
 shares increased by 0.9% on the day and 6.4% on the week. 
 On Friday, the Obama administration announced expanded mortgage-assistance plans to aid unemployed and strapped homeowners in an effort to prevent foreclosures. 
See full story on the expected adjustments to the mortgage-modification program.
/quotes/comstock/13*!xlf
 Another financial overhaul drove shares of SLM Corp. 
/quotes/comstock/13*!slm
, better known as Sallie Mae. SLM shares closed up 4.4% Friday after Congress passed a bill late Thursday to overhaul the nation's student loan program and restrict the role of private lenders. The issue advanced 8.5% for the week. 
 Elsewhere in the financial sector, Progressive Corp. 
/quotes/comstock/13*!pgr
 shares rose 3.7% on analyst upgrades. J.P. Morgan lifted its rating on the stock to neutral from underweight on signs of improvement in the auto insurance sector, and FBR Capital Markets boosted Progressive shares to outperform from market perform. Progressive shares gained 6.5% on the week. 
 Unemployed homeowners will get a few months of relief on their mortgages in a new government assistance plan to help stall more foreclosures. 
Insurer Genworth Financial Inc. 
/quotes/comstock/13*!gnw
 shares increased by 3.8% Friday, for a total gain of 11% for the week. Genworth shares have risen more than 50% so far this year.
 Conversely, Ambac Financial Group Inc. 
/quotes/comstock/13*!abk
 shares lost 21% Friday and 26% for the week, after the International Swaps and Derivatives Association called the seizure by regulators of Ambac's main bond insurance unit a "bankruptcy credit event," prompting Moody's to cut Ambac's senior unsecured debt to C from Ca. 
See more on Ambac's troubles.
 Other bond and mortgage insurers saw gains on the day and week. Radian Group Inc. 
/quotes/comstock/13*!rdn
 shares closed up 6.7% on the day and 23% for the week. Shares of PMI Group Inc. 
/quotes/comstock/13*!pmi
 rose 8.4% Friday and ended the week up 46%. MGIC Investment Corp. 
/quotes/comstock/13*!mtg
 shares rose 13% on the day and 19% for the week. 
 Shares of Flagstar Bancorp Inc. 
/quotes/comstock/13*!fbc
 dropped 38% to close at 57 cents a share Friday after the savings and loan holding company priced a secondary stock offering of 500 million shares at 50 cents a share.
Wallace Witkowski is a MarketWatch news editor in San Francisco.
