, MarketWatch SAN FRANCISCO MarketWatch U.S. financial stocks rose Friday for a fifth consecutive day of gains as financial bellwethers Citigroup Inc. and Bank of America Corp. joined with bond and mortgage insurers to advance the sector by 2 % on the week.The Financial Select Sector SPDR Fund /quotes/comstock/13*!xlf
, which tracks financial stocks on the S&P 500, closed up 0.1% Friday and up 2% on the week.The sector rose 0.4% on the day and 1.4% on the week./quotes/comstock/13*!c

Another financial overhaul drove shares of SLM Corp. /quotes/comstock/13*!slm
, better known as Sallie Mae.SLM shares closed up 4.4% Friday after Congress passed a bill late Thursday to overhaul the nation's student loan program and restrict the role of private lenders.The issue advanced 8.5% for the week.Elsewhere in the financial sector, Progressive Corp. /quotes/comstock/13*!pgr
shares rose 3.7% on analyst upgrades.J.P. Morgan lifted its rating on the stock to neutral from underweight on signs of improvement in the auto insurance sector, and FBR Capital Markets boosted Progressive shares to outperform from market perform.Progressive shares gained 6.5% on the week.Unemployed homeowners will get a few months of relief on their mortgages in a new government assistance plan to help stall more foreclosures.Insurer Genworth Financial Inc. /quotes/comstock/13*!gnw
