 Kimberly-Clark Corporation (NYSE:KMB) rose 2.41% to $62.91 after today it expects 2010 adjusted earnings per share of $4.80 to $5 a share and said marketing spending will continue to increase faster than sales. Analysts polled by FactSet were expecting earnings of $4.82 a share. Through 2015, it expects annual sales growth of 3% to 5%, earnings-per-share growth in the mid- to high-single digits and operating-margin improvements of 0.3 to 0.5 percentage point.
