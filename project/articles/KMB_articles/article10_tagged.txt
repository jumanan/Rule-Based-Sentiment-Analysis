 Shifting Gears: Athletic training gets attention: Former Kimberly Clark wellness expert turns to one on one training

 Do you know when to trade  ?Check for a  from TradingMarkets  APPLETON, Mar 25, 2010 The Post Crescent McClatchy Tribune Information Services via COMTEX Her husband's business transfer with Kimberly Clark Corp. brought Georgia native Theresa Rosenquist to Wisconsin in 1992.A former corporate wellness expert she worked for K C 24 years herself switched to go into the athletic conditioning business.The CrossFit methodology presented a new set of challenges.Q: What you're doing now and what you did as a corporate wellness expert are quite different.Describe the main differences.A: As a corporate trainer I would say things were a little conservative.The difference here is we combine strength with the cardiovascular activities to build both at the same time.The biggest difference is how we combine movements as well as the intensity that we work with those movements.Q: Why did you choose to go this route after corporate wellness?A: I'm so drawn to physical fitness and fitness training.I was originally going to be a K through 12 physical education teacher.So that's just what's carried me through.That's been my love and my passion.I just wanted to stay with it and figured out a way to stay with it.Q: How much does your service cost?A: It's generally about $125 for the month.Q: And for that there are some key differences than, say, from a Y membership?A: I am all hands on, one on one.I coach rather than just lead a class, more of a coach than a personal trainer.Coaching, correcting and motivating.It's really helping someone understand exactly what they need to be doing rather than just going through a motion.There's a purpose and a reason and we're going to fix this until it's right.A: The goal is to keep them healthy and training and not let them get hurt.Q: How did you hear of CrossFit?A: My husband found it.From the minute he did that workout he was hooked.The more I watched what he was doing and the progress he was making was huge.And then I thought this deserves a deeper look.Q: So the regimen is challenging.A: It's hard.However, everything is scalable or modifiable.So I can make it work for just about anybody.I've got clients from 13 years old
