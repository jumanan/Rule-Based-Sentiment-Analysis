Sands' owner moves up on Forbes billionaires list
Of The Morning Call
Sheldon Adelson, founder of the casino company Las Vegas Sands that owns the Sands Casino Resort Bethlehem, bounced back in the ranks on Forbes magazine's annual list of The World's Billionaires.But Chicago real estate investor Sam Zell, who in 2007 took The Morning Call's parent Tribune Co. private in a transaction that resulted in bankruptcy, continued his downward slide on the list of the world's wealthiest people.Adelson's 2010 wealth ballooned to $9.3 billion, ranking him 73 on the Forbes list.That's up from a 2009 net worth of $3.9 million and a rank of 178, a year when the value of Las Vegas Sands plummeted, according to Forbes.Zell's 2010 wealth climbed to $3.8 billion, compared to $3 billion in 2009, according to Forbes.But his 2010 rank slipped to 237 from 205 in 2009, according to Forbes.The number of billionaires climbed to 1,011, up from 793 in 2009, according to Forbes.So Zell slipped as the field of billionaires grew.The world's richest person in 2010, according to Forbes, is Mexican telecom entrepreneur Carlos Slim Helu, with a net worth of $53.5 billion.It is the first time since 1994 that the a non U.S. billionaire was ranked the world's wealthiest person by the magazine.View full article by default
