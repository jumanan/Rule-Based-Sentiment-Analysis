Markets closed higher Monday, with the insurance and pharmaceutical sectors in the spotlight after the House of Representatives passed a health reform bill.The Dow Jones Industrial Average closed up 44 points at 10,786.The S&P 500 rose 6 points to 1,166, and the Nasdaq climbed 21 points to 2,395.An eight session winning run for the Dow came to a halt on Friday after India unexpectedly raised interest rates and as  shares plunged in the wake of a weak sales outlook.The Dow industrials fell by 37 points, the S&P 500 fell five points and the Nasdaq Composite dropped 16 points.The House of Representatives passed President Barack Obama's health care bill over the weekend."While this is clearly an important step, the debate now moves back to the Senate and, with no support from Republicans so far, there is still the possibility of delay as amendments are tabled.There also still remains considerable uncertainty with respect to the use of budget reconciliation to pass reform," said Brian White, analyst at Shore Capital.U.S. health care stocks were wavering as investors digested the vote.Shares of  , the country's largest insurer by members, and  slipped, while Analysts at U.K. broker Panmure Gordon, who follow  , estimate the bill will cut earnings per share by 1.5% to 2.2% each year for the first five years of the bill's enactment.Meanwhile, uncertainty surrounding solutions for Greece's debt woes re emerged ahead of a key summit this week.German Chancellor Angela Merkel told a radio station that markets shouldn't expect a Greek aid package when European leaders gather later this week.Asian markets mostly declined Monday after India's surprise rate increase Friday raised fears of monetary tightening by other regional central banks.Hong Kong fell 2.1%.Reduced expectations of a bailout for Greece, fears that Asian central banks may now start raising interest rates and concern over a series of key events later this week left the euro slightly lower in Europe Monday.The euro was down at $1.3514 from $1.3537 late Friday in New York.The dollar was at 90.54 yen, little changed from 90.56 yen.The pound fell to $1.4996 from $1.5018.Dow Jones Newswires
