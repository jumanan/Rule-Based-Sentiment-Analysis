 (RTTNews) - As the week unfolds, market is slated to witness quarterly earnings reports from few companies, including Family Dollars, Monsanto, Bed Bath & Beyond, Apogee Enterprises, Constellation Brands and Allscripts-Misys Healthcare Solutions. The second quarter of 2010 has opened and in the forthcoming week commencing April 12, Alcoa is scheduled to report quarterly financials, marking the beginning of another season. 
 With no significant companies scheduled to report quarterly earnings on Monday and Tuesday, Wednesday, before the bell, self-service discount stores operator Family Dollar Stores, Inc. ( 
) is slated to report second-quarter results. Wall Street is projecting quarterly profit of $0.78 per share on revenues of $2.09 billion. While announcing second-quarter same store sales, the company reported net sales of $2.09 billion and provided earnings outlook of $0.75 - $0.80 per share. A year ago, the company earned $0.60 per share on revenues of $1.99 billion. 
 Lake Oswego, Oregon-based railcar manufacturer Greenbrier Co. ( 
) is due to publish second-quarter results before the bell, Wednesday, with consensus calling for a loss of $0.23 per share on revenues of $178.25 million. In the earlier year, the company lost $0.41 per share on revenues of $287.13 million. 
 From agricultural products giant Monsanto Co. ( 
), the Street expects earnings of $1.73 per share and revenues of $3.94 billion. The company, which would unveil second-quarter results before the bell on April 07, posted profit of $2.16 per share, on revenues of $4.04 billion, last year.
 Wednesday, after the market closes, electronic health records maker Allscripts-Misys Healthcare Solutions, Inc. ( 
) would report third-quarter numbers. Analysts look for quarterly earnings of $0.16 per share on revenues of $175.28 million. In the previous year, the company earned $0.15 per share on revenues of $163.80 million.
 According to analysts, glass products maker Apogee Enterprises, Inc. ( 
) would post fourth-quarter profit of $0.09 per share on revenues of $163.77 million. Last year, the group earned $0.40 per share on revenues of $201.67 million. The company would report quarterly results after the bell, Wednesday. 
 Home goods retailer Bed Bath & Beyond Inc. ( 
) is set to release fourth-quarter financials after the bell, Wednesday. Analysts anticipate earnings of $0.73 per share on revenues of $2.11 billion, while the company sees earnings of $0.67- $0.71 per share. In the previous year, the group earned $0.55 per share on revenues of $1.92 billion. 
 For the full-year, Bed Bath & Beyond expects earnings per share of $2.11 - $2.15, while consensus calls for earnings of $2.16 per share on revenues of $7.69 billion. In March, FBR Capital Markets "downgraded" the company's shares to "Underperform" from "Market Perform" and lowered its price target to $38 from $38.50. 
 Automotive aftermarket service and retail chain Pep Boys - Manny, Moe & Jack ( 
) is due to unveil fourth-quarter results after the bell, Wednesday, with analysts forecasting loss per share of $0.03 per share on revenues of $476.36 million. A year before, it lost $0.38 per share on revenues of $465.40 million.
 Restaurant chain Ruby Tuesday Inc. ( 
) would post third-quarter profit per share of $0.23 on revenues of $303.71 million, according to Street predictions. Last year, Ruby Tuesday earned $0.26 per share on revenues of $317.52 million. The company would report quarterly earnings after the market closes, Wednesday.
 Thursday, before the market opens, race tracks owner International Speedway Corp. ( 
) is slated to report first-quarter results, with Wall Street looking for earnings of $0.53 per share on revenues of $161.66 million. In the year-ago quarter, the company earned $0.56 per share on revenues of $166.12 million.
 Specialty retailer Pier 1 Imports Inc. ( 
) of Fort Worth, Texas is scheduled to report final quarter results before the bell, Thursday. The company forecasts earnings to be at least $0.29 per share, while analysts look for earnings of $0.31 per share, on revenues of $396.28 million. In the corresponding period last year, the company lost $0.33 per share on revenues of $389.26 million.
 Medina, Ohio-based specialty chemicals company RPM International, Inc. ( 
) is projected to post third-quarter loss per share of $0.07 on revenues of $662.64 million. The company will release quarterly results before the bell, Thursday. In the earlier-year quarter, RPM lost $0.24 per share on revenues of $635.40 million.
 Investors will also focus on final-quarter results from the world's "largest wine company" Constellation Brands, Inc. ( 
, STZ-B), due to report before the bell Friday. The company is expected to earn $0.25 per share on revenues of $735.19 million. The Victor, New York-based group earned $0.21 per share on revenues of $735.10 million.
 For fiscal 2010, the maker of SVEDKA Vodka, Black Velvet Canadian Whisky and Paul Masson Grande Amber Brandy, Robert Mondavi, Ravenswood, Blackstone, Kim Crawford brands, expects earnings of $1.60 - $1.70 per share, while consensus calls for earnings of $1.66 per share on revenues of $3.39 billion.
