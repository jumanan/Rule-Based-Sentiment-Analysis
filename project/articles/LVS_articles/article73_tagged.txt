March 22, 2010, 4:38 PM EDT
More From Businessweek
 March 22 Bloomberg Shares of the following companies had unusual moves in U.S. trading.Stock symbols are in parentheses.Prices are as of 4 p.m. in New York.Health care shares rose after the House of Representatives passed an overhaul of the industry that will ensure tens of millions of uninsured Americans will get medical coverage.Drugmakers gained.Merck & Co. MRK US rose 0.6 percent to $38.30.Pfizer Inc. PFE US climbed 1.4 percent to $17.15.Hospital stocks also advanced.Tenet Healthcare Corp. THC US rallied 9 percent to $6.27 for the biggest increase in the Standard & Poor&rsquo;s 500 Index.Health Management Associates Inc. HMA US climbed 11 percent to $9.05.Community Health Systems Inc. CYH US increased 6.2 percent to $40.51.SunLink Health Systems Inc. SSY US jumped 38 percent to $2.19.Autoliv Inc. ALV US climbed 6 percent to $51.94, the highest price since June 2008.The world&rsquo;s largest maker of automotive seat belts and air bags increased its forecast for first quarter sales and operating margin.Boeing Co. BA US rose 1.7 percent to $71.91.The world&rsquo;s second largest commercial planemaker was raised to &ldquo;outperform&rdquo; at Oppenheimer & Co. and Cowen & Co.Cell Therapeutics Inc. CTIC US plunged 48 percent, the most since March 1998, to 47 cents.The drugmaker failed to win a U.S. panel&rsquo;s backing to sell its experimental drug Pixuvri for aggressive non Hodgkin&rsquo;s lymphoma.GameStop Corp. GME US jumped 2.3 percent to $21.60, the highest price since Jan. 6.The largest U.S. video game retailer may rise to $30 during the next year as growth in its used video game business boosts profit, Barron&rsquo;s reported yesterday, citing Ross Margolies of Stelliam Investment Management in New York.Genuine Parts Co. GPC US rose 3.1 percent to $42.87, the highest price since August 2008.The operator of NAPA auto parts stores was raised to &ldquo;buy&rdquo; from &ldquo;neutral&rdquo; by Goldman Sachs Group Inc.
 Halozyme Therapeutics Inc. HALO US advanced 5.1 percent to $8.46, the highest price since November 2007.The biopharmaceutical company was raised to &ldquo;buy&rdquo; from &ldquo;hold&rdquo; at Brean Murray & Co.
 Las Vegas Sands Corp. LVS US<b><font color='green'> gained 9.9 percent</font></b> to $21.43, the highest price since October 2008.The casino company&rsquo;s share price forecast was raised by $2 to $24 at Sanford C. Bernstein & Co., which said total Macau gambling revenue may jump 30 percent this year, double the firm&rsquo;s earlier 15 percent forecast.Medtronic Inc. MDT US rose 2.3 percent, the most in a week, to $45.80.The biggest maker of heart rhythm devices won a U.S. panel&rsquo;s backing on March 19 to introduce the nation&rsquo;s first heart pacemaker designed to be used safely by patients undergoing magnetic resonance imaging exams.Novell Inc. NOVL US advanced 4.4 percent to $5.89, the biggest gain since March 3.The maker of Linux operating system software said yesterday a $5.75 a share acquisition offer from Elliott Associates LP is &ldquo;inadequate&rdquo; and undervalues the company.Petrohawk Energy Corp. HK US fell 3.7 percent to $19.56, the lowest price since July 10.The oil and natural gas producer was downgraded to &ldquo;neutral&rdquo; from &ldquo;outperform&rdquo; at Macquarie Group Ltd.
 Popular Inc. BPOP US dropped 3.3 percent, the most since Feb. 23, to $2.64.The Puerto Rican bank with branches in the U.S. was downgraded to &ldquo;neutral&rdquo; from &ldquo;buy&rdquo; at B. Riley & Co.
 Williams Sonoma Inc.
