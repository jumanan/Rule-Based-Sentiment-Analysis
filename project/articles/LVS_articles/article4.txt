Stocks Spooked by Greece, DJIA off 28
, April 08, 2010 ) Dallas, Tx - US stocks continued to slide for the third straight day. Today&rsquo;s fall came after an unexpected rise in U.S. jobless claims and worries about Greece's finances, putting the Dow Jones Industrial Average on pace for its longest slide since late January.
 This morning, the Labor Department said initial claims for jobless benefits jumped 18,000 to a seasonally adjusted 460,000. Economists generally say claims must fall to around 400,000 in order to be consistent with a stable labor market.
 On the other hand, Retail stocks and merger talks between two large Airline companies are somehow supporting the broader market after several retailers a better than expected March sales growth.
