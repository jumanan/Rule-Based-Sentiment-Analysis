 Friday, April 2, 2010 Step aside Easter eggs and chocolate bunnies.iPad hits the market this weekend.And it's generating a lot of excitement… perhaps even more so than sugar coated marshmallows shaped like baby chicks.Some estimates even put pre orders alone in the hundreds of thousands.However many fly off the shelves on Saturday, its launch marks the birth of a new computing category.With that said, we might not know these mid sized devices' real potential for months if not years.Even Wall Street doesn't have a clear idea on the subject.When predicting how many of the 9.7 inch screen tablets Apple will sell, those "experts" have guessed everything from 1 million to 10 million.Not very helpful.But while investors clearly can't rely on them in this case, there is information out there that could prove more useful… Several factors could constrain iPad sales, at least in the early days.Even in the longer run, Apple faces competition concerns.While the iPhone debuted well before anybody else had the idea, the iPad will have to fight for its place among similar models likely to hit the market this year.That includes the HP Slate from  .Touted by Steve Ballmer of  , the upcoming Slate runs the touch enabled Windows 7 operating system.It also offers what HP calls "complete internet."
