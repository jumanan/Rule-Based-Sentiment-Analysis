Wall Street Ends Little Changes, Microsoft (NASDAQ: MSFT) Gains

(By Salman - iStockAnalyst Writer)US stocks finished flat on Thursday as weakness in commodity prices overshadowed a rally in technology stocks. Persistent worries over European debt crisis also on sentiment.
 The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 5.06 points or 0.05% to finish at 10,841.21. The S&P 500 slipped 1.99 points or 0.17% to close at 1,165.73. The Nasdaq Composite dropped 1.35 points or 0.06% to 2,397.41.
 Speaking to French Public television, European Central Bank President Jean-Claude Trichet said euro- zone states must be more responsible for their financial situation. "Everything going in the direction of euro zone members shying away from responsibilities is bad in our eyes," he said.
 A release by Department of Labor on Thursday showed number of Americans filing first- time claims for unemployment benefits fell 14,000 to 442,00 in the week ending March 20. Economists expected jobless claims to drop to 457,000. The four-week average of seasonally adjusted initial jobless claims, a less volatile gauge, decreased 11,000 to 453,750.  Continuing claims for the week ending March 13 dropped 345,000 to 5.7 million.
 Appearing before the House Financial Services Committee, Federal Reserve chairman Ben Bernanke said that the central bank continues to anticipate that economic conditions, including low rates of resource utilization, subdued inflation trends, and stable inflation expectations, are likely to warrant exceptionally low levels of the federal funds rate for an extended period.
