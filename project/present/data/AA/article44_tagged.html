U.S. Stock Index Futures Fall; Intel, Alcoa, Lime Energy Drop
March 24, 2010, 8:36 AM EDT
More From Businessweek
March 24 Bloomberg U.S. stock index futures fell on concern government deficits will hamper the global economic recovery as Fitch Ratings cut Portugal’s credit rating and a UBS AG economist said Greece will default on its debt.Intel Corp., the world’s largest semiconductor maker, and Alcoa Inc., the biggest U.S. aluminum producer, slipped in early New York trading.Adobe Systems Inc. climbed 5.5 percent after forecasting second quarter sales that beat analysts’ estimates.ConocoPhillips gained on plans to buy back as much as $5 billion of shares and boost its dividend.S&P 500 futures expiring in June dropped 0.4 percent to 1,165.4 as of 8:32 a.m. in New York.Dow Jones Industrial Average futures lost 0.3 percent to 10,797 and Nasdaq 100 Index futures decreased 0.3 percent to 1,956.5.“The market may not be braced for the level of problems we are facing,” said David Macia, U.S. fund manager at Credit Andorra’s Credi Invest unit in Andorra La Vella, Andorra.His firm oversees $5.7 billion.“Something that should not have a big impact, such as a downgrade of Portugal, will rattle the market.The problem of fiscal shortfalls will go on for some time.We view the current macro state in Europe with a high level of caution.”
Fitch Ratings downgraded Portugal’s long term foreign and local currency issuer default ratings to “AA ” from “AA,” citing the nation’s deteriorating public finances.The outlook remains “negative,” Fitch said, meaning its more likely to cut the grade further than raise it or keep it unchanged.Default
Separately, Paul Donovan, UBS Investment Bank’s deputy head of global economics, said Greece “is going to default at some point,” and Europe’s failure to answer that challenge will hurt its common currency.U.S. stocks advanced yesterday as signs of growing demand for steel and semiconductors boosted confidence the economic recovery is strengthening.The S&P 500 has rallied 74 percent from a 12 year low in March 2009 as the economy returned to growth and a record long slump in earnings ended.The aggregate profit for companies in the S&P 500 increased in the fourth quarter from the year earlier period for the first time since the second quarter of 2007.Analysts estimate first quarter earnings will grow 30 percent, according to a Bloomberg survey.Intel slipped 0.2 percent to $22.62.Alcoa lost 0.5 percent to $14.43 in early New York trading.‘Taking a Breather’
“We’re taking a breather,” said Clemence Bounaix, who helps oversee about $4.5 billion at KBL Richelieu Gestion in Paris.“Earnings didn’t offer a lot of guidance.Economic statistics haven’t been that great.We remain cautious.”
Lime Energy Co. sank 17 percent to $4.29.The provider of energy efficiency engineering posted a fourth quarter loss excluding some items of 17 cents a share, wider than the 7 cent estimate from analysts in a Bloomberg survey.Adobe Systems jumped 5.5 percent to $37.15 in New York.The world’s biggest maker of graphic design programs forecast second quarter sales that beat analysts’ estimates, a sign the company is poised to release an upgrade of its most profitable software.USEC Inc. gained 4.9 percent to $5.62.The largest supplier of enriched uranium to U.S. nuclear reactors said it will receive $45 million in funding from the Department of Energy for centrifuge technology.MF Global Holdings Ltd. rallied 15 percent to $8.39.Former New Jersey Governor Jon Corzine, who ran Goldman Sachs Group Inc. from 1994 to 1999, has accepted an offer to lead the futures and options broker.With assistance from Alexis Xydias in London.Editor: Roger Neill, Michael P.
